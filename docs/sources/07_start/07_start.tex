    \section{Week 7 - Nonlinear Dynamics}\label{week-7---nonlinear-dynamics}

We have now built enough tools to tackle some challenging physical
systems that have nonlinear equations of motion. The broader field of
study that deals with these systems is called
\href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonlinear_system}{Nonlinear
Dynamics}. Nonlinear dynamics or nonlinear science is the study of
systems that are described by nonlinear equations. These systems are
often chaotic, meaning they are highly sensitive to initial conditions.
In many cases, these systems are treated using
\href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynamical_system}{dynamical systems
theory}.

Nonlinear dynamics is the science that has helped us understand the
dynamics of
\href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Navier\%E2\%80\%93Stokes_equations}{fluids},
\href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kadomtsev\%E2\%80\%93Petviashvili_equation}{water
waves}, \href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonlinear_optics}{crystals
and other optical systems},
\href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Li\%C3\%A9nard_equation}{radio
waves},
\href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Numerical_weather_prediction}{the
weather}, and countless other systems. It has been used in
\href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turbulence}{fluid dynamics},
\href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plasma_modeling}{plasma physics},
\href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Double_pendulum}{mechanical
systems}, and many other fields.
\href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chaos_theory}{Chaos theory} is
associated with nonlinear dynamics and is a subset of the field.
Recently, this work has extended to quantum systems, where the
Hamiltonian is nonlinear.
\href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_chaos}{Quantum chaos} is a
field that studies the quantum analog of classical chaotic systems.

    \subsection{Nonlinear Differential
Equations}\label{nonlinear-differential-equations}

Differential equations are the language of classical mechanics. They
describe how the systems we investigate evolve over time. They track the
\href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_variable}{state variables} of
the system, which are what define the system. For us, this is often the
position components and the velocity components of the system. The video
below gives a good overview of what differential equations are and why
they are important for our study. It also provides an introduction to
the concept of a \href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phase_space}{phase
space}, which is the space defined by all the state variables of the
system.

\subsection{Phase Portraits}\label{phase-portraits}

Solving non-linear equations can be quite difficult, and in most cases,
we can't find an analytical solution. We can numerically integrate the
equations to find specific trajectories. But this is a fairly
inefficient approach, especially when nonlinear systems exhibit such
complex behavior and are so sensitive to initial conditions.

We can also use a technique called
\href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phase_portrait}{phase portraits} to
understand the behavior of these systems. A phase portrait is a
graphical representation of the trajectories of a dynamical system in
the phase space. That space is defined by the combination of all the
state variables of the system. For example, a simple pendulum has two
state variables: the angle and the angular velocity. The phase portrait
of the pendulum is a plot of the angular velocity against the angle. Or
for the harmonic oscillator, the phase portrait is a plot of the
velocity against the position.

\emph{Phase portraits are quite useful for second order differential
equations (or any two-dimensional system) because we frequently use and
interpret 2D graphs.} Notice that we focused on ``2nd order differential
equations'' not ``linear 2nd order.'' That is because, as we will see,
\textbf{phase portraits are particularly useful for nonlinear 2nd order
differential equations.}

We will go into the details of how to construct and develop phase
portraits in class. This video from
\href{https://www.me.washington.edu/facultyfinder/steve-brunton}{Steve
Brunton} is a good overview of the process. It's quite detailed and
takes a mathematical perspective, so don't worry if you don't understand
everything in the video. We have plenty of time to investigate how this
works in practice.
